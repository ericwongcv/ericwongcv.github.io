FBACalculator

Android Application

Google Play Store Link

Background

Before App Developement

During my sophomore year in college, I decided to start selling old textbooks that were sitting on the shelves to make some side income. The platform I found to be best suited for my purpose was Amazon.com. Once I started selling more and more books, I decided to grow my business and start expanding and selling other products. As I looked for potential products, I had to figure out which ones were profitable to sell. Aside from checking competitor pricing, I had to find out whether or not I can make a profit after accounting for product cost, shipping fees, and most importantly, Amazon Seller Fees.

Problem

Reason for Development

Caculating Amazon fees was always a hassle because there is no way to estimate seller fees in Amazon's provided apps, such as the Amazon Seller app. The only way to calculate the fees was to go into my Amazon seller account on a desktop browser and navigate through a series of pages before finally getting to an Amazon fees estimation page. This wasn't a good method for me because I never had a computer with internet access by me when I was out looking for products to sell. Even if I did have the required tools, it would be too complex for something that should be extremely simple. At this point, I was only able to note down products and prices to calculate at a later time. So I started to search for another solution. I went straight to the mobile app store and only found one potential application for my purpose, but the app was developed for Amazon sellers in a different country so it wasn't something that would work for me.

Solution

Process Improvement

Without an existing solution to my problem, I decided to create one. The goal was to create a product that is simple to use, and only contained what is necessary to meet my objective. I first thought about writing a program with MATLAB to calculate the Amazon fees since MATLAB was a program I was already familiar with. If I went this route, the app wouldn't be reliant on the internet, but I would still have to carry a computer with me when picking out products to sell. So I decided to write a local application (Independent of the internet) on a mobile platform instead. This was the best solution because being a local application, the app would always be swift no matter what my network speed was, and I would always have access to it in my pocket.

Not having any experience in mobile application development, I decided to research and learn the process of app development on the Android platform. I reasearched anything that I needed to make this app a reality. It took approximately two weeks to gain the necessary knowlege. And afterwards, another week to develop and publish the final product, the FBACalculator.

Result

Outcome

Using the new application I developed, I was able to find and list much more products at a time compared to the one or two while using Amazon's estimator. This resulted in a tremendous increase in profit
