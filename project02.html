Background

Before Product Design

My family decided to purchase a security camera system for our house one day. It was meant to be used as a remote system where we would only be able to view the cameras through our mobile devices. But we eventually decided that we should have a dedicated monitor that is always on so we would be able to view the camera immediately if inside the house. Since we didn't have a spare monitor for the system, I decided to take apart a monitor from an old broken laptop and use it as the dedicated monitor for our security system. For this to work, I also needed a video controller board to allow inputs to the screen, so I purchased that part seperately. Once the controller board came, there was another problem. I didn't have anything to hold the monitor up in place.

Problem

Reason for Product Design

I created a temporary solution by putting two pieces of wooden boards in a T-shape. I used wood because it was non-conductive so it wouldn't damage the circuit board. It was also sturdy enough to hold the monitor up. This solution was functional but had its own problems.

It held up the monitor at a good angle and was functional as intended, but the circuit boards were slowly collecting dust. This could damage the boards in the long-term. It was also unsafe to leave the boards exposed, so I decided to design an enclosure for it.

Solution

Improved Product

I started the enclosure design by taking some measurements with a caliper, then taking notes and drawing sketches on paper. Afterwards, I worked on the enclosure design on Solidworks following the information on my sketches and notes. When the final design was completed, I printed it on a 3D printer.

Upper Lid, Layer Shift and Warping


Other problems occurred while printing on a 3D printer. The print failed frequently due to layer shifting and warping. After tinkering with the 3D printer and print files, the warping problem was fixed, but layer shifting still occurred. I decided to go ahead and try putting the top and bottom pieces together to check the fit. The result turned out well with all the parts in place, including the controller board and monitor.

Result

Outcome

The circuit boards are now protected from dust and exposure, all buttons and ports are accessible, and the monitor is held up in place. Additionally, it is easier to move the setup to different locations in the house compared to before. The enclosure and stand has held up the monitor for over 4 months and is still currently being used.
