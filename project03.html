Background

Before Product Design

I always carried a set of keys in my pocket with a standard keyring holding all the keys together. But it became inconvenient when I had to put my keys in its own pocket in order to prevent it from damaging the other stuff I was carrying. So I started looking for key holders to solve this problem.

Problem

Reason for Product Design

After some time searching, I found a keyholder that would work. It was a swiss knife type of key holder. It prevented my keys from shifting around in my pocket and kept them well organized.

Hide

First Keyholder

Swiss Knife Key Holder

I used it for quite some time until a period where I frequently went grocery shopping. When I came back home, I always held a bag of groceries in one hand, leaving the other hand free to access my keys. When I took the key holder out, I wouldn't be able to get the right key because they would always stick together so flipping out one key would pull other ones with it. So I decided to design my own key holder that would suit my specific requirements.

Solution

Improved Product

Product Design First Draft


After drawing these sketches, I began designing a rough draft of the keyholder using Solidworks. I did a test print of the rough draft to check if the tolerances were correct. Overall it was functional, but I felt that the edges were too sharp, and maybe slightly greater tolerances might work better due to printing inaccuracies. I went back to Solidworks to correct these features and did a final print of the design.

Result

Outcome

Besides the difficult time removing support material, the final product turned out very well. All pieces fit perfectly and the product works as intended. The keys were accessible with one hand, the key holder kept my keys organized, and it also fit well in my pocket.

After using the key holder for over a month, it still holds up well, but there are a few changes that would make it even better. The first and simplest is to reduce the bulk of the design. While adding the curved edge features, I also increased the bulk. Reducing it would make the product look much better. Second, a better locking mechanism may be required for the key holder. After some amount of taking the keys in and out, the back piece became loose and falls out with light pressure. Lastly, a better method should be implemented to lock the keys in place. Currently, some of the keys pop out of its slot just from the force of gravity. Fixing these issues would result in a much better key holder.
